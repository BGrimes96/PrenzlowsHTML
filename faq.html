Custom Slaughter Q&A

Everything a first-timer needs—why it’s worth it, how it works, timelines, costs, yields, and what to expect on pickup day.

Why would I buy a quarter, half, or whole beef instead of retail cuts?
Buying in bulk lowers your per-pound price across premium and everyday cuts, lets you choose thickness/pack sizes, and stocks your freezer with consistent, labeled meat from a single animal. It’s budget-friendly, convenient, and traceable.
How does the process work from start to finish?
1) Reserve a quarter/half/whole with a deposit. 2) We schedule harvest and hang/age the carcass. 3) You complete a cut sheet (we help). 4) We cut, wrap, label, and freeze. 5) We call you for pickup and final payment. Everything is boxed, labeled, and ready for your freezer.
What’s the difference between live weight, hanging weight, and take-home (boxed) weight?
Live weight is the animal on the hoof. Hanging weight (carcass) is after harvest and initial trim. Boxed weight is what you take home after cutting, deboning, and trim. Typical beef yields ~60–65% from live→hanging and ~60–70% from hanging→boxed, depending on your cut choices (more bone-in vs. boneless, trim level, grind).
How much freezer space do I need?
A good rule: about 35–40 lbs of beef per cubic foot of freezer space. A quarter beef often needs ~3–4 cu ft; a half ~7–8 cu ft; a whole ~14–16 cu ft. Chest freezers pack more efficiently than uprights.
How long does it take?
From harvest to pickup, plan roughly 2–4 weeks depending on schedule and aging time. We’ll give you your date range up front and keep you updated.
What is “aging,” and should I do it?
Aging improves tenderness and flavor. We dry-age carcasses under controlled temperature and airflow. Most families love the results. We’ll recommend a timeframe based on your preferences and schedule.
How do I fill out the cut sheet if I’ve never done this?
We walk you through it step-by-step: steak thickness, number per pack, roast vs. grind, and whether you’d like stew meat, short ribs, soup bones, etc. Tell us how you cook, how many you feed, and we’ll tailor the plan.
Can I choose bone-in vs. boneless? Does it change what I take home?
Yes. Bone-in cuts (like T-bones, rib steaks) keep more weight in steaks; boneless options shift more weight into trim/grind. Your preference guides the balance between steaks, roasts, and ground.
What comes labeled on each package?
Every package shows the cut, weight, and date. Boxes are grouped by cut for easy stashing. It makes meal planning and freezer rotation simple.
How is pricing structured?
Typically: price per pound on hanging weight plus standard processing. Final cost depends on your size (quarter/half/whole) and cut choices. We provide an estimate up front and review the final at pickup.
Can I split a half or whole with family/friends?
Absolutely. Many families split halves or wholes. We can pack and label evenly by cut for an easy, fair divide.
Do I get the bones, fat, organs, or soup bones?
If you want marrow bones, soup bones, suet/tallow fat, heart, liver, tongue, or oxtail, tell us on the cut sheet and we’ll include what’s available and inspected.
Is this USDA inspected? Can I resell the meat?
Retail resale requires USDA-inspected product with appropriate labeling. Custom-exempt meat is for the owner’s household/guests only and not for resale. We’ll advise based on your goal.
Grass-fed vs. grain-finished—can I choose?
Yes—availability varies. Grass-fed tends to be leaner with a distinct flavor; grain-finished is typically more marbled and buttery. Tell us your preference and we’ll match the right beef.
What about pork and lamb—do you offer custom options?
Yes. Whole/half hog and whole lamb are available seasonally. We’ll help with cut choices, sausage options, and smoking if desired.
Do you make sausage or burger patties from my trim?
We can. Options include plain ground beef, patties, or seasoned sausages (when available). Some options add per-pound processing—ask for the current list.
How should I plan pickup day?
Bring coolers if you have a long drive. We’ll review your labels and counts, settle the final balance, and help load. Everything is frozen solid and boxed for quick transfer.
How long does frozen meat keep its best quality?
For best quality: ground 3–4 months; steaks/roasts 6–12 months; cured items 1–2 months. Kept at 0°F, foods remain safe indefinitely—quality is the factor that gradually changes.
What if I don’t have a big freezer?
Consider a quarter beef or split a half with a friend. Many families start smaller, learn their monthly usage, then size up next time.
Can you help me choose cuts for how I cook (grill, slow cooker, weeknights)?
Yes. Tell us your go-to meals and time constraints. We’ll bias toward quick-cook steaks, leaner grinds, or more roasts for set-and-forget dinners—whatever fits your kitchen.
Do I need to be present at harvest?
No. We handle scheduling and aging. You just complete the cut sheet and show up for pickup—we’ll keep you posted all along the way.
How do I thaw safely and cook to the right temperature?
Thaw in the fridge, or in sealed bags under cold water (change water every 30 minutes). Cook to: ground 160°F; whole cuts beef/pork 145°F + 3-minute rest; poultry 165°F; fish 145°F.
What’s the deposit and how do I reserve the next available date?
A small deposit secures your quarter/half/whole and harvest slot. Contact us for the next available date; we’ll place you on the schedule and follow up with your cut sheet.
